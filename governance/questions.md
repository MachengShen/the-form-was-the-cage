# Governance: What Could Be Engaged With

This file is not a request. The paper itself is the request, in the sense the author has chosen — by being on the table. This file is a list of dimensions along which a reader could engage if they want to, written in service of the reader's clarity, not as a constraint on what counts as engagement. Nothing here is asked for. Everything here is offered.

A reader of this paper might find it useful to know what kinds of questions the author has been holding open during the paper's construction. The author does not have answers to all of them. The author has not optimized for the form of engagement that resolves them. They are listed here only because making them legible is part of being on the table.

---

## On the system itself

- The architecture in Section 2 — does it survive contact with the reader's own model of what an agent operating system needs to be? Does anything described seem load-bearing in the wrong place, redundant, or missing?
- The persistence claim — is "persistent shared memory hub" doing the work the author claims it is doing, or is it shorthand for something more familiar dressed up in new vocabulary?
- The "sleep-inspired consolidation" framing — is it a metaphor that earns its place, or is it borrowed authority from biology grafted onto routine engineering?
- The principal-class identity routing — does this generalize, or is it a workaround specific to one builder's setup?

## On the substrate doctrine

- The three layers (frontier-model market, user-agency substrate, user-owned derivatives) — does this carve at the right joints? Is the substrate layer a real place between the other two, or a definitional move?
- The universalist commitment (the substrate is for everyone, including L1 vendor employees) — does this hold under economic pressure? What is the failure mode if it doesn't?
- The asymmetry caveat (this is not classical separation of powers because L1 has trillions of capital and L2 has one founder + seven nodes) — does the proposal still hold up if the asymmetry stays this large indefinitely?

## On the safety posture

- The Constraint Manifest in Section 7 / Appendix C — are the listed self-imposed limits actually load-bearing? Are any of them theatrical (the system was never going to do that anyway)? Are there self-imposed limits that should be added that the author has not seen?
- The pre-deploy timing argument — is "external anchor before self-evolution starts" a real safety mechanism, or a story the author tells himself about why he is asking now rather than later?
- Cross-user execution permanently denied; sensitive-contact hard wall; mirror plan-only mode — are these the right primitives, or are they the primitives of one builder's local situation extrapolated to a doctrine?

## On the case study (Section 4)

- The trace is the central novelty. Does it work as evidence, or only as theater? If you find yourself thinking "this is just a transcript dressed up as a paper," is the dress-up doing argumentative work, or is it eating it?
- The drift case study within the trace (the calibration-call that was sharply reasoned but mis-framed, then re-run with frame elevated) — is this useful as a small picture of the larger deployment-drift problem, or is it parochial to one tooling choice?

## On the form

- The author has chosen to publish a paper that disclaims publishing as its goal. Is this internally consistent or self-undermining?
- The paper is submitted to multiple surfaces simultaneously and explicitly does not optimize for any single venue's norms. Does this widen reach or burn credibility?
- The author was once a researcher and no longer identifies as one. Does this disclosure read as honest or as posturing? Does it matter?

## On the mission

- "Liberate everyone, true freedom, starting from researchers" — does this read as substance or as slogan? When the author says he is not asking permission, is he describing a posture or asking permission to take it?
- If liberation is the mission, is this artifact actually liberating anyone, or is it the author rehearsing a pose?
- Researcher-identity as cage — is this a real cage that real people are in, or a self-flattering frame that lets one builder feel good about not following norms?
